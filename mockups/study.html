SN1 vs SN2

SN2 reactions happen in a single step: the nucleophile attacks the backbone carbon at the same time the leaving group departs, causing an inversion of configuration (like an umbrella flipping inside out).

SN1 reactions instead proceed in two steps — the leaving group departs first, forming a carbocation intermediate, and the nucleophile attacks afterward. This is why SN1 reactions favor more substituted (more stable) carbocations.
